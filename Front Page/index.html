The Smallest Light

It is a quiet flame, barely a flicker,
Not the roaring fire others dream of.
But in my hands, it glows steady,
Enough to ward off shadows that linger.


Your words are stones skipped on still water,
Ripples soft, fleeting—but they reach.
To some, they're only pebbles,
To me, a map across an endless sea.


They say it's nothing, a passing breeze,
The kind you forget before it settles.
But I have stood in airless rooms,
Where even a sigh would feel like a gift.


What you offer fits no grand measure,
No towering edifice to point and praise.
Yet it fills the hollow spaces I carry,
A rhythm, small but constant, in my chest.


Not all hearts weigh the world the same;
Some are shaped by what they've lacked.
And even the smallest light feels like dawn,
When you've only ever known the dark.


Parallel Lines

Wandering 'neath the heavens, lost in circles,
Still, I cannot draw near, alas, I strive.
I turn mine eyes from thee, though deep within
I long for thee to know the ache I bear.
Yet, the closer I draw, the further thou dost seem.
What more, I ask, can be done?
The hearts, Oh, how they stretch, and grow farther still.


As parallel lines that never touch nor meet,
We circle, we spin, but never to unite.
Yet I swear, I shall not yield,
For in time, we shall be one,
Together, forevermore, in wait I stand.


Alas, I failed to speak, to make thee know,
That I do love thee, with all my heart, so true.
My dreams, they flutter like the snowflakes fall,
A miracle borne on winter's breath.
If only I could race through time,
And grow, so as to understand,
How much thou dost mean to me, I would take thy hand.


And still we cross each other's path,
Though fate doth keep us from the words we seek.
I watch thee there, hesitant, unsure,
As if the world itself could hold us back.
Yet we remain, forever young,
Living as we did in days of yore.


Though we may lose our way,
Trapped within the wrong of fate's cruel hand,
We shall, by providence, find one another again.
And thou shalt know my love,
And in my heart, thou shalt believe.
Though I am not yet ready, still I go.


I swear to thee, by moon and sun,
Thou shalt stay by me, and never change,
With smiles that shine like stars in snow,
I shall never let thee go.
Now, in time, I shall run through the years,
And grow, to understand,
How much thou dost mean to me,
And in thy hand, mine shall remain.
